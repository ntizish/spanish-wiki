# La Diada Nacional de Catalunya

**Type:** National Day · **Date:** September 11 · **Region:** Catalonia

Catalonia's national day. Not a celebration of independence but a day of remembrance — September 11, 1714 is the date Barcelona fell to Bourbon forces, ending the War of the Spanish Succession and abolishing Catalan institutions. It has become the primary annual demonstration of Catalan identity, and since 2012, of pro-independence sentiment.

## What Happened on September 11, 1714

The War of the Spanish Succession (1701–1714) was a European conflict over who would inherit the Spanish throne. Catalonia backed the Habsburg claimant (Archduke Charles of Austria) against the Bourbon Philip V. When the war ended with the Treaty of Utrecht (1713), Catalonia was abandoned by its allies.

Barcelona held out under siege until September 11, 1714, when Bourbon forces breached the walls. The aftermath:
- **Nova Planta decrees** abolished the Catalan Courts (Corts) and most Catalan institutions
- Catalan was suppressed as a language of public life
- The **Ciutadella fortress** was built to control Barcelona — demolishing an entire neighborhood to do so
- **Rafael Casanova**, the city's conseller en cap (chief counsellor), led the final defense and was wounded; he became a symbol of resistance

## The Monument: Rafael Casanova

The statue of [[people/Rafael Casanova|Rafael Casanova]] on Carrer d'Alí Bei in Barcelona is the focal point of La Diada. Thousands of Catalans lay flowers at the statue each September 11.

## Modern La Diada

For most of the 20th century, La Diada was a quiet commemoration. Since 2012 it has become the largest annual political demonstration in Europe:

- **2012** — 1.5 million people in Barcelona; called the first modern mass pro-independence rally
- **2013** — "Via Catalana": a 400km human chain across Catalonia; ~1.6 million participants
- **2014** — V-shaped human formation in Barcelona
- **2017** — Came weeks before the October 1 independence referendum and subsequent Spanish government response
- **Post-2017** — Demonstrations continue but with more internal debate about strategy

## The Politics

La Diada is a Generalitat (Catalan government) public holiday. It is celebrated by Catalans of many political persuasions — some see it purely as cultural commemoration, others as a pro-independence rally. The two readings coexist uneasily.

## Notes

The date "September 11" is coincidental with the 2001 attacks — Catalan nationalists sometimes note this causes confusion internationally. The Catalan date has been commemorated since at least the early 20th century, long before 2001.
